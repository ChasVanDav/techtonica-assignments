Vanessa's Kimchi Recipe

Kimchi, or spicy fermented cabbage, is a food staple in every Korean kitchen. Just before the grueling winter months, Koreans gather as a family and make large batches of Kimchi that last until the spring. This gathering is called 'Kimjang,' a tradition passed down for a thousand years. Similar to saurkraut or pickles, kimchi is a great tangy accompaniment to any heavy or greasy meal. Since it is a fermented food, it takes some time to prepare. However, it's well worth the wait and I guarantee you'll be making it time and time again.

    Ingredients
  • Napa cabbage
  • Julienned asian radish
  • Minced asian pear
  • Gochu powder
  • Coarse salt
  • Minced garlic
    Instructions
  1. Cut napa cabbage in half, lengthwise and spread them out
  2. Gently rub coarse salt between each cabbage leaf, being sure to use enough salt
  3. Wait 3 hours, drain the cabbage water, and rinse the salt off of theleaves
  4. Make a spicy paste by mixing minced garlic, gochu powder, and minced pear
  5. Add julienned radish to spicy paste
  6. Rub each cabbage leaf with spicy paste
  7. Leave to ferment in a dark, cool place for 2 days
  8. Refrigerate for 1 day
  9. Enjoy alone or with hot rice
